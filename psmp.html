PUBLIC SPEAKING MENTORING PROGRAMME (PSMP)

The Public Speaking Mentoring Program, abbreviated as PSMP is designed to introduce mentees to the field of professional public speaking and empower them with excellent public speaking skills that will make them stand out in any public speaking engagement. PSMP is a preparatory course that will successfully launch mentees into the art of professional public speaking.

Register Now

Objectives of PSMP

  • To raise brilliant public speakers through direct professional training and mentorship.
  • To teach mentees the practical and theoretical components of public speaking.
  • To help mentees overcome glossophobia (fear of public speaking).
  • To enhance the public speaking and presentation skills of mentees.
  • To prepare mentees for a rewarding career in professional public speaking.
  • To help aspiring public speakers jump-start a career in public speaking the easy way, amongst others.

PSMP Learning Outcomes

  • Mentees will learn practically how to overcome glossophobia (fear of public speaking) and improve their confidence in delivering speeches.
  • The communication and public speaking skills of mentees will improve remarkably.
  • Mentees will learn how to apply their theoretical knowledge of public speaking in real life public speaking situations.
  • Mentees will learn how to engage the different speech styles in public speaking in different speaking situations.
  • Mentees will learn the basic essentials involved in speech preparation and delivery.
  • Mentees will learn how to effectively deliver the four basic types of speech presentation.
  • Mentees will learn how to use storytelling in public speaking.
  • Mentees will learn how to arrest an audience attention with a presentation.
  • Mentees will learn the theoretical and practical component of public speaking.
  • Mentees will learn how to professionally evaluate a speech presentation.
  • Mentees will learn how to become more effective with conversations, amongst others.

PSMP Duration/Class activaties

The duration of PSMP is 8 Saturdays. PSMP class runs from 12pm-3pm.

  • Teaching/mentoring session
  • Q & A session.
  • Interactive session.
  • Practical session.
